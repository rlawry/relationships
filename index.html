How to Read These Graphs

Look at the shape first, then ignore whether the axes are flipped. A flipped graph can look strange, but the relationship type is still determined by whether the graph is straight or curved, whether it rises or falls, and whether it goes through the origin.

Proportional

Look for a graph that goes through the origin and keeps the same overall ratio. In this game, some proportional relationships are straight lines and some are curved through the origin. If it starts at zero and stays tied to zero, think proportional.

Linear (nonproportional)

Look for a straight line that does not go through the origin. It rises at a steady rate, but it has a starting amount already built in.

Negative linear/opposite

Look for a straight line that slopes downward. As x increases, y decreases. The line is still straight, but the variables move in opposite directions.

Nonlinear

Look for a curve instead of a straight line. The rate of change is not constant. If the graph bends and does not simply make a straight rising or falling line, it is nonlinear.

When the axes are flipped

Do not let the axis direction trick you. A flipped axis can move the picture around, but it does not change whether the graph is straight or curved, or whether the variables move together or in opposite directions.
